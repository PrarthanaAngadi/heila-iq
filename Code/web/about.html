Heila Technologies is an MIT startup with the mission of enabling simple and intelligent power. Our flagship product, Heila IQ, is a decentralized control platform that allows for plug&play deployment and remote optimized operation of microgrids.

The Heila IQ was designed by MIT students and alumni with expertise on microgrids, control, computer science, and machine learning to make building and managing microgrids easier.

Heila Technologies Announced Winner of the US Department of Energy Cleantech University Prize. Prize will accelerate the development of next generation plug n’ play microgrids

Denver, June 6, 2016 - Heila Technologies was announced as the first prize winner of the 2016 Cleantech University Prize, organized by the United States Department of Energy. The $100,000 award includes a $50,000 monetary prize as well as a voucher to access facilities at the world class DOE National Labs to enable further development of Heila’s microgrid control platform.

“As distributed renewables grow in importance, microgrids are becoming critical in allowing for much higher renewable energy penetration. Microgrids are already increasing the resilience of critical infrastructure electricity systems,” said Jorge Elizondo, PhD of Electrical Engineering and Co-Founder of Heila Technologies.

“Today building a microgrid is a long and expensive process – every component speaks a different language and requires a customized solution, much like internet communication protocols decades ago. With Heila’s microgrid controller, disparate energy resources and loads can be automatically integrated and optimized, enabling plug n' play deployment of microgrids,” explained Elizondo. The Massachusetts Institute of Technology team has already shipped a prototype product to its first customer in California, and was previously awarded the $100,000 Grand Prize of the MIT Clean Energy Prize competition.

By utilizing proprietary hardware and software, Heila IQ reduces deployment time and costs, and adds critical intelligence capabilities to today’s microgrids. The control platform has near-term applications at military bases, critical infrastructure, and local communities.

The all-MIT team includes Jorge Elizondo Ph.D and John Donnal Ph.D, Francisco Morocz (MS MechE ’14), Aly Eltayeb (MBA and Ph.D Chemical Engineering Candidate), and Albert Chan (MBA ’15, MS Engineering Systems ‘15).
